IUPUI- Causality Project

We are a team working under NSF Award Number 1948322: “Capturing Dynamism in Causal Relationships: A New Paradigm for Relationship Extraction from Text”. The principal investigator is Dr. Sunandan Chakraborty, a data science professor at Indiana University-Purdue University of Indianapolis (IUPUI). Our team consists of both graduate and undergraduate research assistants in the school of informatics at IUPUI.

In the causality project, we mainly focus on natural language processing, specifically causality relation detection and extraction from text data sourced from the web. Another goal is exploring unsupervised or semi-supervised techniques for the use of natural language processing. Finding a way to reliably source causal information in an unsupervised or semi-supervised matter can help in the advances of downstream applications.

During 2022, two papers were submitted to two different conferences for review. The first one was over causal relation extraction using semi-supervised deep learning models, and how a modified version of pointwise mutual information could potentially be used to correlate to causal strength.

The second paper was on causal relation detection and model explainability, which is looking at what the model deems important for this binary classification task. Another focus of this paper was creating a dataset of explicit marked and implicit unmarked causal statements. The model was trained on the explicit causality and tested on implicit causality to see how well the knowledge transfer was. This could be used in curating a more balanced causal dataset which is often overlooked in currently available datasets.
